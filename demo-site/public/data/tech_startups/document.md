# 6 Trends In Tech And Startups For 2026

Source: Crunchbase News (https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/2026-tech-startup-trends-ipo-ai-ma/)
Published: January 30, 2026
Author: Marlize van Romburgh

The venture capital landscape in 2025 was the third-strongest global venture funding year on record. Looking ahead to 2026, six key trends are shaping the tech startup ecosystem.

## Trend 1: Strong IPO Market

After a four-year slowdown, public markets rebounded in 2025 with 23 or more US billion-dollar IPOs, compared to just 9 in 2024, totaling over 125 billion dollars in valuations at IPO pricing. Companies like CoreWeave, Figma, and Navan successfully went public. Aman Singh, corporate partner at Fenwick and West, was involved in all three IPOs. Experts expect continued momentum through 2026, with profitable AI companies positioned as strong candidates. Databricks and Cohere are among potential 2026 IPO candidates, alongside fintech unicorns Plaid and Revolut.

## Trend 2: M&A Activity Surge

Approximately 2,300 venture-backed startup M&A deals closed in 2025. The largest venture-backed acquisition was Google's purchase of Wiz for 32 billion dollars. Healthy IPO markets typically increase rather than reduce M&A activity. Trends include AI acqui-hire activity and stalled unicorn exits. George Mathew, managing director at Insight Partners, noted that increased M&A activity is expected as larger companies look to acquire AI capabilities.

## Trend 3: Concentrated Funding Growth

Venture funding is expected to increase 10 to 25 percent year over year. Capital is flowing primarily to AI, robotics, and defense tech sectors, while climate tech, crypto, and undifferentiated vertical AI are expected to struggle. Tim Tully, partner at Menlo Ventures, sees early-stage mega-rounds and established high-growth companies as the primary beneficiaries. Jordan Leites, VP at Norwest Venture Partners, noted that capital efficiency has become the key differentiator.

## Trend 4: Capital Concentration and Bubble Concerns

The top 5 companies, OpenAI, Scale AI, Anthropic, Project Prometheus, and xAI, raised 84 billion dollars in 2025, representing 20 percent of all venture funding that year. OpenAI alone raised 40 billion dollars in the largest private funding round ever. SpaceX reached an 800 billion dollar valuation, the highest for a private company. Anuj Bahal, KPMG US technology sector leader, warned about unsustainable concentration and circular deal-making. Lukas Hoebarth of EY-Parthenon expressed concerns about whether current AI investment levels are sustainable.

## Trend 5: AI-Driven Workforce Transformation

Approximately 55,000 US tech layoffs in 2025 cited AI as a contributing factor. Marc Benioff, CEO of Salesforce, cut the workforce from 9,000 to 5,000 employees, citing AI productivity gains. Microsoft and Amazon also made significant AI-related layoffs. This trend is expected to accelerate in 2026 as AI tools become more capable at replacing routine engineering and support tasks.

## Trend 6: Fintech Rebound

Fintech funding jumped 27 percent year over year to 51.8 billion dollars in 2025. Amias Gerety, partner at QED Investors and head of US investments, sees growth in stablecoins, agentic payments, and AI-native financial tools. Pre-IPO companies and AI-enhanced financial services are anticipated as growth drivers. The regulatory environment under the new administration is viewed as more favorable for fintech innovation.

## Key Market Players

The AI compute infrastructure market is dominated by Nvidia and Oracle, while the foundation model layer is contested between OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and xAI. Enterprise AI adoption is being driven by Salesforce, Microsoft, and Amazon. The defense tech sector has emerged as a significant new category, with companies building AI applications for national security.

Venture capital firms actively shaping the landscape include Insight Partners, Menlo Ventures, Norwest Venture Partners, QED Investors, and Fenwick and West on the legal advisory side. The intersection of AI and fintech represents one of the most dynamic convergence points heading into 2026.
